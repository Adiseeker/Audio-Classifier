- **Opportunities**:

  - Improving international cooperation could help address global pollution more equitably.
- **Risks**:
  - Rapid shifts in energy policies without adequate support could lead to an economic crisis, increasing poverty and social unrest.
  - Ignoring the health impacts of air pollution could result in further loss of life and increased healthcare costs.

**Conclusion:**
The text raises urgent concerns about the economic, environmental, and social implications of current energy policies in Poland. It suggests that while transitioning to cleaner energy sources is necessary for long-term sustainability, the approach must be balanced with measures to support the economy and mitigate potential health crises. Ignoring these interconnected challenges could lead to a severe economic crisis and significant societal harm.

It is imperative for policymakers to address these issues holistically, considering both short-term and long-term impacts, and ensuring that transitions are supported by appropriate economic and social policies.

### Key Themes

1. **Entrepreneurship in Poland**: The text discusses the historical and current status of entrepreneurship in Poland, highlighting both positive attitudes towards business and challenges faced by entrepreneurs.
2. **Media Impact on Perception**: It explores how media coverage affects public perception of entrepreneurship and its influence on the decision to start or close businesses.
3. **Economic Conditions**: The text delves into economic factors, including complex business regulations and support from the state, which impact the success and failure rates of businesses in Poland.

### Impacts

1. **Economic Dimension**: The decrease in new company formations and increase in business closures suggests a stagnant economy. High complexity in business rules and lack of government support are factors contributing to this trend.
2. **Technological Dimension**: Although not explicitly mentioned, the implications of economic stagnation can hinder technological advancements and innovations that new businesses might bring.
3. **Political Dimension**: The low ranking in business-friendly conditions indicates a need for political reforms to simplify regulations and improve support systems for entrepreneurs.
4. **Social Dimension**: The change in media perception has social repercussions, influencing public attitudes towards entrepreneurship and potentially discouraging new ventures.

### Examples and Evidence

1. **Entrepreneurial Spirit**: Poland ranks high globally in positive attitudes towards business ownership (78% of Poles believe running a business is good).
2. **Media Influence**: Between 2016 and 2019, the percentage of positive media coverage on entrepreneurship dropped from 58% to 37%.
3. **Business Complexity**: Poland ranks second in Europe and tenth globally for having complex business regulations, according to the Global Business Complexity Index 2021.
4. **Declining Entrepreneurship**: The index of people running young companies has decreased from 9% to 5.4% over nine years, while mature businesses have increased their share from 5% to 12.8%.

### Opportunities and Risks

**Opportunities:**
1. **Government Reforms**: Simplifying business regulations could encourage new startups and boost the economy.
2. **Public Campaigns**: Positive media campaigns could revive enthusiasm for entrepreneurship.
3. **Support Systems**: Implementing better support systems for young businesses can help them thrive.

**Risks:**
1. **Economic Downturn**: The current trend may lead to a long-term economic slowdown if not addressed.
2. **Brain Drain**: Talented individuals might seek opportunities abroad due to unfavorable business conditions.
3. **Innovation Stagnation**: A decrease in new businesses could hinder technological advancements and innovations.

### Conclusion

The text underscores the need for comprehensive reforms to support Polish entrepreneurship. Simplifying business regulations, improving government support, and promoting positive media narratives can reverse the trend of declining new ventures and stimulate economic growth. Future considerations should include monitoring and addressing external factors like energy increases, which exacerbate current challenges.

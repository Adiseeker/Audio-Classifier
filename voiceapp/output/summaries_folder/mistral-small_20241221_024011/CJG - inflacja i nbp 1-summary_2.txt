### Impacts
1. **Economic Impact**: High inflation rates can devalue currency, making goods and services more expensive for consumers and reducing the purchasing power of households. This is especially problematic in poorly managed economies where governments may use high inflation to pay off internal debts.
2. **Technological Impact**: While not directly mentioned, technological advancements could be hindered by economic instability caused by high inflation rates, as businesses might face higher costs and reduced consumer spending.
3. **Political Impact**: Governments under financial stress may resort to manipulating economic data to appear more stable, potentially leading to political unrest if the public perceives this deception.
4. **Social Impact**: High inflation rates can exacerbate social inequalities by punishing hard-working individuals and rewarding those who benefit from economic instability. This can lead to social unrest and decreased trust in government institutions.

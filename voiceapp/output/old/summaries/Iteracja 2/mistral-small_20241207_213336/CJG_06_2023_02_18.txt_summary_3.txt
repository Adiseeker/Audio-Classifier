 ### Summary

#### 1. Key Themes
- **Economic Development**: The text discusses the role of cheap energy in economic development and the consequences of making energy expensive.
- **Environmental Concerns**: It highlights health issues related to air pollution, specifically mentioning deaths caused by high concentrations of particulate matter.
- **Political and Moral Hypocrisy**: The author questions the moral consistency of political actions, citing examples like China's human rights abuses.
- **Economic Self-Harm**: The text argues that Poland is harming its own economy by increasing energy costs without a corresponding global effort to reduce pollution.

- **Technological**: No direct technological impacts are mentioned, but there is an implication that technological advancements might be hindered by high energy costs.
- **Political**: The text implies political hypocrisy in dealing with environmental issues, with stronger countries like China and the USA being the biggest polluters while Poland bears the brunt of economic sacrifice.

- **Opportunities**:
  - **Moral Consistency in Policies**: If Poland adopts more consistent moral and environmental policies, it could potentially reduce internal contradictions and hypocrisy.
  - **Global Cooperation**: By pushing for global cooperation on reducing pollution, Poland could align its economic interests with environmental goals without bearing the brunt alone.
- **Risks**:

  - **Health Crises**: Continued high levels of air pollution pose significant health risks to the population.
  - **Political Backlash**: Pursuing more consistent policies might face political resistance or backlash from influential global players.

#### 5. Conclusion
The text paints a grim picture of Poland's future if current economic and environmental policies continue unchanged. It calls for urgent action to address the moral inconsistencies in global environmental policies and to mitigate the risks of an impending economic crisis. The author emphasizes the need for a more balanced approach that considers both economic development and environmental sustainability, advocating for global cooperation to achieve these goals effectively.

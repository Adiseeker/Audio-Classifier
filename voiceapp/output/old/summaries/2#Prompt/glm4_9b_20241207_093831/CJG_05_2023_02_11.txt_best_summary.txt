### Key Themes:
1. **Entrepreneurial Activity Decline**: The decreasing number of new businesses being established in Poland.
2. **Entrepreneurial Attitude and Respect**: The admiration for entrepreneurs globally and particularly among Poles.
3. **Economic Conditions**: The challenging business environment, including complexity of rules and media portrayal.
4. **Comparative Analysis**: Comparison between Poland's business conditions with those of other European countries, specifically Switzerland.

### Impacts:
1. **Economic**: Decreased entrepreneurship could impact economic growth due to the crucial role of small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs).
2. **Technological**: A lack of new businesses may slow down technological advancements as innovation often comes from new ventures.
3. **Political**: The political perception of business friendliness in Europe with Switzerland leading as a pro-entrepreneurial country.
4. **Social**: Negative impacts on social mobility and job creation if new businesses are not encouraged.

### Examples and Evidence:
1. **Entrepreneurial Attitude**: Poland ranks high globally for believing entrepreneurship is a good idea, yet the percentage of Poles willing to start a business has significantly dropped.
2. **Media Perception**: The decline in positive media portrayals of entrepreneurs from 58% in 2016 to 37% in 2019, as per CBOS studies.
3. **Business Complexity**: Poland ranks highly on the Global Business Complexity Index, indicating it is one of the hardest countries for running a business.

### Opportunities and Risks:
1. **Opportunities**: For the government to reform regulations, promote entrepreneurship, and provide more support to new businesses.
2. **Risks**: Without improvements, the decline in entrepreneurship could lead to economic stagnation and exacerbate job shortages.

### Conclusion:
The text highlights a concerning trend in Poland where despite a strong entrepreneurial spirit and global recognition, there is a decrease in new business formations. The complex regulatory environment and negative media portrayal are cited as key factors contributing to this issue. The government needs to take steps to foster a more supportive ecosystem for entrepreneurship to prevent economic setbacks and promote growth. Future considerations should include regulation reform, state support, and positive media representation of entrepreneurs to reverse the trend observed.
